The Untapped Resource

India holds a sleeping giant
in plain sight

The Palmyra palm — Borassus flabellifer — is one of India's oldest, most resilient trees. It survives floods, droughts and extreme heat with zero agricultural inputs. Yet for generations, its extraordinary fibre potential has remained commercially unexploited. Until now.

100 Year Lifespan

A single palmyra tree produces a century of fibre — no replanting, no replanting costs. An eternal, self-sustaining supply chain that makes our raw material virtually free at scale.

15-20 Years to First Yield

Once mature, palmyra trees yield prolifically for 80 continuous years — providing predictable, decades-long supply security that no seasonal crop can match.

∞ Zero-Effort Harvest

Fruits fall naturally by their own weight. No mechanical picking, no intensive labour. Nature performs the harvesting — our workforce simply collects what the tree gifts them.

0 Waste Generated

Post-extraction seeds are cultivated into saplings, regenerating the very groves that feed our supply chain. A true spiral economy — every by-product becomes tomorrow's asset.

"We are not simply launching a new textile. We are unlocking a resource that has endured for centuries — deploying it with 21st-century fibre science, and returning its value directly to the women who have tended these trees for generations."
